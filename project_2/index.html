Tips and Tricks for the Budget Traveler:

Europe is expensive. If you must go to Europe, try a country that is further east, like Hungary, Serbia, Paris or the Czech Republic. These Countries aren't as developed as their western neighbors, which means less tourism infrastructure. In some of these places, beer is cheaper than water.


Go in the off season. If you have your heart set on finding your true love on top of the Eiffel Tower, try visiting during the off season. Sure, it may not be super warm, but many major cities have stellar holiday seasons. Travel will be considerably more inexpensive, you won't have to wait in line forever for major attractions, and you will see a different side of the city than the #popular instagram posts.


Stay at hostels or an Airbnb. Hostels are a fraction of the cost of hotels and are often filled with young travels who are in the exact same position that you are. Do your research. Places like Hostel World are great sites to compare your options. Airbnb is also a great choice for the thrifty wanderer. It is a great way to really learn what it's like to live in a city.


Leave the Harry Potter books at home. Packing light will be your biggest ally. If at all possible, bring just a carry on with you, preferrably a backpacking pack. Not only does this save money on the flight over, but it is so much easier to navigate foreign cities and hostels if you aren't worrying about carrying all of your earthly possesions. Bring the essentials, and if you need something, you can always find a place to replenish your supplies.
